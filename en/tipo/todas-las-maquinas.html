Full-Machine Pilates in Valencia

Studios in Valencia with all Pilates apparatus: reformer, Cadillac, chair, barrel and more. The most complete experience of the Pilates method.

Full-machine Pilates studios have the complete apparatus developed by Joseph Pilates: reformer, Cadillac (trapeze table), Wunda chair, spine corrector and barrels. Each piece of equipment targets different muscle groups and movement patterns.

This format offers the most complete experience of the Pilates method. Sessions are usually individual or semi-private (2–3 people), allowing the instructor to design a personalised programme that progresses through all the apparatus.

Ideal for: People who already have some Pilates experience and want to deepen their practice, or those seeking a more complete, classical approach to the method.

Benefits

  • Complete, varied training across all apparatus
  • Greater progression and variety of exercises
  • Highly personalised — individual or semi-private sessions
  • Faithful to the original Pilates method
  • Ideal for advanced practitioners seeking depth
  • Works the whole body from multiple angles

What is full-machine pilates?

Full-machine studios (also called 'comprehensive' or 'classical' Pilates) have the complete range of apparatus developed by Joseph Pilates: reformer, Cadillac (trapeze table), Wunda chair, barrels and more. This allows a wider variety of exercises, greater progression and a more complete experience of the original method.

Frequently asked questions

What machines does a full-machine Pilates studio have?

A complete studio has reformer, Cadillac (trapeze table), Wunda chair, spine corrector, barrels and other apparatus designed by Joseph Pilates. Each piece works different movement patterns and muscle groups.

Is full-machine Pilates more expensive?

Generally yes, as sessions are usually individual or semi-private and the studio investment is much higher. Expect to pay between €30 and €60 per session in Valencia.

Do I need experience to do full-machine Pilates?

Not necessarily, though many studios recommend starting with reformer or mat classes to build a foundation before moving on to all the apparatus.

What is the difference between full-machine Pilates and reformer-only?

Reformer-only studios focus exclusively on the reformer machine. Full-machine studios have all the apparatus, allowing a wider variety of exercises and a more complete experience of the original Pilates method.
